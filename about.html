PLATINUMPRESS

Platinum Press was built in Fort Worth, Texas around a simple conviction: pharmaceutical packaging should be produced with the same rigor as the product it protects.

That conviction shaped everything that followed. cGMP-compliant facilities. ISO 9001:2015 quality systems. G7 Master color. Two Koenig and Bauer Rapida presses, the same class of press trusted to print the world's currency. And a rule that never bends: 100% in-line vision inspection on every unit, every run.

We stayed privately held on purpose. No layers between you and the people who own the outcome, senior attention on every job and the agility to move at your program's speed.

Today, Platinum Press is a single-source packaging partner supporting more than 100 pharma and healthcare brands across the United States.

Our clients fueled that growth: labels, folding cartons, literature, foil lidding, flexible packaging and security products, consolidated under one roof, one standard and one accountable team.

The $15M headquarters expansion adds the capacity to keep 3-4 week lead times at national volume, with 2-day shipping to 90% of the US.

FAQ

About Platinum Press, Answered

Is Platinum Press privately held? −
Yes, and deliberately so. Private ownership keeps senior attention on every job, direct accountability for quality and the agility to move at your program's speed, without the layers of a public conglomerate.
Is Platinum Press an American manufacturer? +
Yes. Platinum Press operates two US facilities, headquartered in Fort Worth, Texas, centrally located to reach 90% of the United States within 2-day shipping.
What certifications does Platinum Press hold? +
Our facilities operate under cGMP and hold ISO 9001:2015, G7 Master and SGS&Co certifications, with 100% in-line vision inspection across every program. Certificates are available on request.
How can I learn if Platinum Press fits our packaging program? +
Contact Sales with your product, formats and timeline. A packaging expert will review the program and respond within one business day, with samples and a technical review as the next step.
