01 · Foundation

Premium. Established. Trustworthy.

Platinum Press competes with public giants and wins on quality, but loses on perception. Every design decision in this system exists to close that gap: the site must look as big, as certified and as capable as the company actually is. Compliance proof leads, scale follows, craft is visible everywhere.

The one-line test: if a layout, color or copy choice makes Platinum Press look smaller, cheaper or less certified than a public competitor, it is wrong, no matter how nice it looks.
Logo: the existing Platinum Press logo is off-limits for redesign. Until final logo files are delivered, all designs use the text lockup PLATINUMPRESS set in Geist 600, with PRESS at 75% opacity.

08 · Voice & Copy

How Platinum Press Speaks

Proof-led, calm, specific. Every claim carries a number, a certification or a named process. The reader is a procurement, quality or brand professional who is paid to be skeptical.

RuleDetail
Five-second message"A trusted pharmaceutical packaging partner for labels, cartons and literature, with quality and compliance." Every hero is a variation of this.
Primary CTAContact Sales, everywhere: nav, section CTAs, big CTA band, footer. Repeat it wherever a buying decision could happen. "Get a Quote" is demoted.
Proof over adjectivesWrite "100% in-line vision inspection", not "exceptional quality". The sanctioned proof points: cGMP, ISO 9001:2015, G7 Master, SGS&Co, 100% inspection, $15M expansion, 2 facilities, two K&B Rapida presses, 100+ brands, 3 to 4 week lead, 2-day shipping to 90% of the US.
No em dashesNever use em dashes in site copy: headlines, body, CTAs, meta, alt text. Use commas, periods, parentheses or restructure the sentence.
Sentence-style headlinesHeadlines read as statements with periods: "Quality You Can Audit. Compliance You Can Prove." Title Case, full stops allowed.
Attribution policyUntil real client quotes are cleared, quotes attribute to Platinum Press teams (Quality Systems, Serialization Team, Press Operations). Never invent client names, people or logos.
Privately-held angleLean into it: senior attention, agility, accountability, "the public giants" as the implicit contrast. Never punch down by name.

10 · Do & Don't

The Short List

✓ Do

  • Lead every page with compliance and proof, then scale, then craft
  • Keep display type at weight 400 and let size carry hierarchy
  • Use one blue for all actions and Ice for all dark-surface accents
  • Alternate white, cream and navy sections for rhythm
  • Reuse the sticky rail, dark link list and stat tile patterns
  • Use real Platinum Press photography with the three sanctioned treatments
  • Repeat Contact Sales wherever a decision could happen

✕ Don't

  • Don't bold hero headlines or tighten line-height below 1.06
  • Don't introduce new colors, gradients or radii
  • Don't use stock photos, renders or invented client logos and quotes
  • Don't use em dashes anywhere in site copy
  • Don't mix light and dark systems inside one component
  • Don't use Action Blue decoratively; it means "clickable"
  • Don't redraw or restyle the Platinum Press logo
